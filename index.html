ABOUT ME

Hello! I'm Tristan, a software engineer based in Berlin, Germany.

I enjoy creating things that live on the internet, whether that be websites, applications, or anything in between. My goal is to provide helpful tools and if they can be combined with some data analysis and visualization my inner scientist is happy.

By training I am a computational scientist with a Bachelor and Masters degree in Bioinformatics. After graduating from the Eberhard Karls University of Tübingen, I joined the bioinformatics group of the German Center for neurodegenerative diseases where I also had written my thesis. I stayed in the lab for 6 months to finish some follow up experiments and tie some other loose ends. As a next step I moved to the exiting capital of Germany, Berlin, where I joined and currently still are, the Charpentier Lab, under the leadership of nobel price winner Emanuelle Charpentier.

During these days of data analysis, I discovered web development as a creative hobby and I love it ever since! It is a completely different world than my scientific everyday life in that you have completely different methods, possibilities and results, and I try to learn as much as possible from both.

In my free time I try to do as much sport as possible, mostly in the form of running, in 2021 I want to run my first half marathon, or cycling here in the flat surroundings of Berlin. Apart from that, I like to do body-weight training and currently try my hand at calisthenics. Since I am also an enthusiastic scuba diver, most of my larger trips take place in Southeast Asia, where the density of incredible dive sites is the highest. Some of my favorite spots are probably the Komodo National Park in Indonesia and Apo Island in the Philippines. As a South German, I have a special bond to mountains, I love to spend the winters on the ski slopes and I'm also very much into hiking in general. Currently I am just arriving back home from a hiking vacation in Denmark!
